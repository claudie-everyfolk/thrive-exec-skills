# Prior Quarter Earnings Notes — Q4 FY2025

## Opening Remarks (CEO)

Thank you for joining us today. Q4 was a strong finish to fiscal 2025 for Thrive Market. We delivered revenue of $192.4 million, up 16% year-over-year, driven by continued membership growth and the early traction of our fresh category pilot.

Our membership base reached 1.52 million active members at quarter end, representing a 14% year-over-year increase. More importantly, our annual retention rate improved to 71%, up from 68% in Q3 — a direct result of the investments we've made in member experience and the auto-ship program.

## Financial Highlights (CFO)

Revenue of $192.4 million exceeded our guidance range of $185-190 million, driven by:
- Membership revenue of $28.8 million, up 18% YoY as we crossed 1.5 million active members
- Merchandise revenue of $163.6 million, up 15% YoY with strong holiday season performance

Gross margin came in at 28.2%, down 80 basis points sequentially due to:
- Promotional activity during the holiday season (Black Friday, holiday gift sets)
- Higher mix of lower-margin pantry staples as new members stocked up
- Partially offset by improved vendor terms negotiated in Q3

Adjusted EBITDA was $11.5 million (6.0% margin), compared to $10.2 million (6.1% margin) in Q3. The slight margin compression reflects our intentional investment in the fresh category pilot and fulfillment automation.

Operating expenses were $41.8 million:
- Marketing spend of $14.2 million (7.4% of revenue) — slightly above our target of 7.0% due to holiday member acquisition campaigns
- Technology investment of $9.8 million reflecting the fresh category platform build and OMS migration
- G&A of $8.2 million, largely flat quarter-over-quarter

## Key Themes Discussed

### Fresh Category Pilot
We launched fresh produce and refrigerated items in Los Angeles and San Francisco Bay Area in October. Early results exceeded expectations:
- Fresh orders represent 8% of total orders in pilot markets
- Fresh customers place 2.4x more orders per month than non-fresh members
- Fresh AOV is $68 compared to $56 for non-fresh orders
- Customer NPS for fresh is 72 — highest across all categories

We plan to expand fresh to 3-5 additional metros in Q1 FY2026.

### Fulfillment Efficiency
Fulfillment cost per order was $8.42, down from $8.91 in Q3:
- Warehouse automation (robotic picking in Batesville, IN) contributing 6% throughput improvement
- Route optimization reducing last-mile delivery costs
- We expect to reach $8.00 per order by end of FY2026

### Membership & Retention
Annual membership retention improved to 71% (up from 68% in Q3):
- Auto-ship program driving stickiness — 31% of orders now auto-ship
- Personalized recommendations increasing reorder rates
- New "Thrive Perks" loyalty tier launched in November showing early positive signals

### Unit Economics
We shared an updated view of member lifetime value:
- Average member LTV is $385 over a 3-year horizon
- Member acquisition cost (MAC) averaged $32 in Q4
- LTV/MAC ratio of 12.0x — a strong signal that our growth investments are generating returns

## Guidance

For Q1 FY2026, we expect:
- Revenue of $195-200 million
- Adjusted EBITDA margin of 5.5-6.0%
- Net new members of 40,000-50,000
- Fresh category expansion to 3 additional metros

For the full year FY2026:
- Revenue of $830-860 million
- Adjusted EBITDA margin of 6.0-6.5%
- Active membership exceeding 1.7 million

## Analyst Q&A Highlights

**Q: What gives you confidence in the fresh category unit economics long-term?**
A: We're seeing significantly higher order frequency and AOV from fresh members. While cold chain logistics are currently margin-dilutive, we expect to reach contribution margin breakeven in fresh by Q3 FY2026 as we scale volume and optimize our cold chain network.

**Q: How should we think about the path to profitability?**
A: Our model is inherently leverage-able. Membership revenue is essentially 100% margin, and as we scale, fulfillment costs per order continue to decline. We believe we can reach EBITDA margins of 8-10% at scale while continuing to invest in growth.

**Q: What's the competitive moat for Thrive vs. Amazon or Instacart?**
A: Our membership model creates fundamentally different economics. Amazon and Instacart are transaction-based — they need to win every order. Our members are locked in annually and shop with us 4-5 times per month. The organic/natural focus also means our assortment is curated, not infinite — which our members actually prefer.
