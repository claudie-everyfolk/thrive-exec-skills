# Q1 FY2026 Business Themes

These are the key narratives we want to communicate to investors this quarter. All commentary and Q&A responses should reinforce these themes.

---

## Theme 1: Fresh Category Scaling

**The story:** We expanded fresh produce and refrigerated items from 2 pilot metros (LA, SF Bay Area) to 5 metros (adding Phoenix, Denver, Austin) in Q1. Fresh is our highest-conviction growth bet.

**Key proof points:**
- Fresh now available in 5 metros covering ~18% of our member base
- Fresh orders grew to 12% of total orders in pilot markets (up from 8% in Q4)
- Fresh members ordering 2.6x more frequently than non-fresh (up from 2.4x)
- Fresh AOV increased to $72 (up from $68 in Q4)
- New metro ramp is tracking ahead of the LA/SF pilot at the same stage
- Cold chain cost per order improving: $14.50 in new metros vs. $11.80 in mature metros (was $14.00 in early pilot)

**What we're NOT ready to say:**
- Don't commit to a specific national rollout timeline yet
- Don't share fresh category gross margin — it's still negative and improving
- Don't make promises about fresh subscription bundles (still in testing)

---

## Theme 2: Membership Retention Improvements

**The story:** Retention is the single most important metric for our model. After plateauing at 68% for three quarters, we've inflected to 72% and have clear line of sight to 75%+ by year end.

**Key proof points:**
- Annual retention rate: 72% (up from 71% in Q4, 68% in Q2-Q3)
- Auto-ship penetration: 34% of orders (up from 31% in Q4)
- Members on auto-ship retain at 86% vs. 61% for non-auto-ship
- Thrive Perks loyalty tier: 42,000 members enrolled since November launch
- Perks members show 2.1x higher 90-day retention vs. control group
- Churn reason analysis: #1 reason shifted from "price" to "product selection" — a healthier signal (fixable with assortment)

**What we're NOT ready to say:**
- Don't share LTV projections beyond the previously disclosed 3-year horizon
- Don't commit to a specific retention target publicly (75% is internal)
- Don't break out retention by cohort vintage in detail (some early cohorts look weak)

---

## Theme 3: Fulfillment Center Automation

**The story:** Our Batesville, IN fulfillment center is our automation proving ground. The robotic picking deployment is working, and we're planning to roll it out to our second facility.

**Key proof points:**
- Fulfillment cost per order: $8.18 (down from $8.42 in Q4, target $8.00 by EOY)
- Robotic picking now handling 35% of SKUs in Batesville (up from 22% in Q4)
- Throughput improvement: 12% more units per labor hour vs. Q4
- Error rate on robotic picks: 0.3% vs. 1.8% for manual picks
- Planning Phase 1 automation deployment at Reno, NV facility in Q3
- Estimated $4.2M annual savings at full Batesville automation; $3.8M projected for Reno

**What we're NOT ready to say:**
- Don't disclose total CapEx for automation program (board hasn't approved Reno yet)
- Don't commit to a third facility automation timeline
- Don't share specific vendor names for robotics partners

---

## Overarching Narrative

Thrive Market is transitioning from a growth-stage e-commerce company to a scalable, profitable platform. The three themes this quarter — fresh expansion, retention inflection, and automation savings — are the three legs of that story. Each one independently improves our unit economics, and together they create a compounding flywheel: better selection (fresh) drives higher retention, higher retention drives more predictable volume, and more volume drives automation ROI.

The message to investors: we're not choosing between growth and profitability. We're building the infrastructure that delivers both.
