# Business Context Notes — March 2026

Prepared by: FP&A Team
Period: March 2026 (Month 3 of FY2026)

## Revenue Drivers

- **Spring Wellness Promotion (Mar 1-15):** Ran a 25% off sitewide promotion on vitamins, supplements, and wellness products. Drove higher-than-expected order volume but at lower margin. Estimated incremental revenue of $1.8M but margin dilution of ~400bps on promoted categories.
- **Fresh Category Expansion:** Launched fresh produce in 3 new metro areas (Phoenix, Denver, Austin) on March 10. Early results strong — fresh AOV is $72 vs. $58 overall. Incremental fulfillment costs still ramping.
- **Membership Growth:** Added 18,200 new annual members in March (budget: 15,000). Acquisition cost per member came in at $28 vs. budget of $32 due to strong organic/referral mix. Membership revenue recognized ratably.
- **Auto-Ship Penetration:** Auto-ship orders now represent 34% of total orders (up from 31% in Feb). Higher retention signal, slightly lower AOV per order.

## Cost Drivers

- **Fulfillment Center Staffing:** Hired 45 temporary warehouse associates for spring surge (budget assumed 30). Overtime hours also above plan due to the wellness promotion driving 22% more picks per day than forecast.
- **Carrier Rate Increase:** FedEx implemented a 4.2% general rate increase effective March 1. Not fully reflected in our AOP. Estimated $180K incremental shipping cost for the month.
- **Packaging Material Costs:** Corrugated cardboard prices up 8% from Q4 due to recycled fiber supply constraints. Added ~$95K to COGS vs. budget.
- **Refrigerated Shipping for Fresh:** Cold chain logistics for the fresh expansion running at $14.50 per order vs. budget of $11.00. Volume still below breakeven threshold for the new metros.

## Headcount & People

- **Tech Team Backfills:** Filled 3 senior engineering roles that had been open since January. Salary expense coming in $45K above budget for the month due to market adjustments.
- **Marketing Team Restructure:** Eliminated 2 performance marketing coordinator roles; reallocated budget to influencer partnerships. Savings of $22K in salary, offset by $85K in new influencer contract commitments (hitting Marketing Expense).
- **Customer Support Scaling:** Added 8 part-time support agents to handle fresh category inquiries. Cost neutral against budget as we reduced outsourced support vendor spend.

## One-Time Items

- **Warehouse Lease Renegotiation:** Recorded a $320K one-time benefit from renegotiated lease terms at our Batesville, IN fulfillment center. Hits Occupancy & Facilities line.
- **Software Migration:** $175K one-time expense for migrating from legacy OMS to new order management platform. Hits Technology & Infrastructure.
- **Legal Settlement:** Accrued $250K for settlement of a vendor contract dispute. Hits G&A — Legal & Professional.
