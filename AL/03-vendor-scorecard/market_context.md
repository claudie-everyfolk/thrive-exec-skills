# Market Context & Thrive Strategic Priorities

*Prepared for Q2 2026 JBP Season*

## Category Trends (Natural & Organic Grocery)

### Growing Categories
- **Organic Snacks:** +12% YoY across natural channel. Driven by better-for-you reformulations and portion-controlled formats. Thrive outperforming channel at +14%.
- **Clean Beauty & Personal Care:** +19% YoY. Fastest-growing non-food category. Consumers increasingly applying "clean label" food standards to personal care. Ingredient transparency is the #1 purchase driver.
- **Functional Supplements:** +16% YoY. Probiotics and gut health leading. GLP-1-adjacent supplements (fiber, protein, metabolic support) emerging fast.
- **Frozen & Fresh:** +22% YoY for Thrive specifically (new cold chain infrastructure). National natural channel growing at +9%. Significant headroom.
- **Bone Broth & Functional Broths:** +14% YoY. Crossing from health niche to mainstream pantry staple.

### Plateauing Categories
- **Plant-Based Protein (Meat Alternatives):** +2% YoY, down from +18% two years ago. Consumer fatigue with ultra-processed plant proteins. Shift toward whole-food plant-based (beans, lentils, tempeh) and away from engineered alternatives.
- **Kombucha:** +1% YoY. Market saturation. Innovation shifting to functional sodas and adaptogenic drinks.
- **Traditional Supplements (Multivitamins):** +3% YoY. Growth shifting from generic multis to targeted, condition-specific formulas.

### Declining Categories
- **Conventional Cereal:** -4% YoY in natural channel. Consumers shifting to higher-protein, lower-sugar breakfast options. Granola and oatmeal holding steady.
- **Flavored Water:** -6% YoY. Losing share to functional beverages and sparkling water with adaptogens.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Amazon (Whole Foods + Amazon Fresh):** Aggressive on price, expanding private label organic. Thrive's differentiation: curation, member trust, ingredient standards. Amazon doesn't vet ingredients -- they sell everything.
- **Costco (Kirkland Organic):** Growing organic private label. Price leader on pantry staples. Thrive counters with assortment depth and specialty brands Costco won't carry.
- **Sprouts Farmers Market:** Expanding nationally. Similar customer base. Thrive's advantage: e-commerce convenience, membership model, broader standards.
- **Direct-to-Consumer brands:** Many Thrive vendors also sell DTC. Key negotiation point: Thrive drives discovery and volume they can't get on their own site.

## Thrive Market Strategic Priorities (2026)

### 1. Expand Fresh & Frozen
- Cold chain infrastructure investment paying off. Need to grow fresh/frozen from 12% to 20% of GMV by end of year.
- Priority: onboard 15+ new fresh/frozen vendors, improve instock rates in cold chain (currently lagging at 86% vs. 94% ambient target).
- **Vendor implication:** Fresh/frozen vendors get favorable terms and prominent placement. Ambient vendors should expect pressure to innovate or accept tighter margins.

### 2. Improve Instock Rate to 95%+
- Current platform average: 93.4%. Target: 95.0%.
- Top offenders: Meat & Seafood (86-88%), Personal Care (93.6%), Pasta & Sauces (91.2%).
- **Vendor implication:** Instock rate is now a JBP scorecard metric. Vendors below 93% will face performance improvement requirements. Fill rate below 90% triggers vendor review.

### 3. Reduce Vendor Concentration
- Top 5 vendors represent 38% of total GMV. Target: reduce to 30% by diversifying within categories.
- **Vendor implication:** Large vendors should expect Thrive to onboard competitors. Negotiation leverage increases for Thrive. Counter: large vendors can offer exclusives and innovation partnerships.

### 4. Margin Improvement
- Target: 50 basis point improvement in blended margin (from 37.2% to 37.7%).
- Levers: negotiate better terms with top 12 vendors, shift mix toward higher-margin categories (supplements, personal care, cooking oils), reduce promotional depth on low-margin items.
- **Vendor implication:** Expect margin conversations in every JBP. Vendors growing fast with Thrive will have less pricing pressure. Flat/declining vendors will face margin asks.

### 5. GLP-1 Member Segment
- 34% of members report being on or considering GLP-1 medications. Fastest-growing member segment.
- Need dedicated merchandising, product curation, and vendor partnerships targeting this audience.
- **Vendor implication:** Vendors with GLP-1-compatible products (high protein, low sugar, portion-controlled, whole foods) get premium placement and co-marketing support.

## Key Negotiation Themes for This JBP Cycle

1. **Instock accountability** -- vendors below benchmark must commit to improvement plans with measurable targets
2. **Margin improvement** -- 25-50 bps improvement ask for mature vendors; faster-growing vendors get more flexibility
3. **Innovation pipeline** -- every vendor should present 2-3 new items aligned with Thrive trends (GLP-1, clean beauty crossover, fresh/frozen)
4. **Promotional efficiency** -- shift from deep discounts to strategic promotions (bundles, thresholds, new member trials)
5. **Data sharing** -- push for more vendor-side data sharing (sell-through at other retailers, consumer insights) in exchange for Thrive platform data
