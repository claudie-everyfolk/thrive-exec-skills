# Vendor Scorecard & JBP Prep

## Context Frame

I'm April Lane, Chief Merchant at Thrive Market. My Category Managers spend 5-8 hours preparing for each vendor Joint Business Plan (JBP) meeting -- pulling performance data, benchmarking against category averages, reviewing last year's negotiation outcomes, and assembling talking points. We have 12 key vendor meetings coming up and I need a systematic way to prep for all of them at once.

Each JBP is a strategic conversation: we're reviewing the vendor's performance on our platform, discussing growth opportunities, negotiating terms, and aligning on the next year's plan. The quality of our prep directly impacts the terms we get and the partnerships we build.

## The Ask

Using the attached files -- `vendor_performance.csv` (12 vendor performance profiles), `market_context.md` (category trends and Thrive strategic priorities), and `negotiation_history.json` (prior year negotiation details for 3 key vendors) -- please:

1. **Generate a one-page vendor scorecard for each of the 12 vendors.** Each scorecard should include: overall performance grade (A/B/C/D), key metrics vs. category benchmarks, trend arrows (improving/stable/declining based on YoY data), and risk flags (any metric significantly below benchmark). Format these consistently so they're comparable side-by-side.

2. **Write JBP talking points for each vendor** covering: leverage points (where Thrive has negotiating strength), partnership opportunities (where mutual growth is possible), areas of concern (underperformance, reliability issues), and a recommended tone for the meeting (collaborative, firm, exploratory, etc.).

3. **For the 3 vendors with negotiation history** (in the JSON file), produce a detailed negotiation prep brief: what we asked for last year, what we got, what changed since then, recommended positions for this year, data-backed arguments for each ask, and walk-away points.

4. **Priority-rank all 12 vendor meetings** from most to least important. Factor in: revenue size, growth trajectory, strategic alignment with Thrive priorities, unresolved issues from prior years, and potential upside from renegotiation. Include a one-line rationale for each ranking.

5. **Identify cross-vendor patterns** -- are there systemic issues (e.g., multiple vendors with declining fill rates)? Category-level trends that affect multiple vendors? Opportunities to use one vendor's performance as leverage in another's negotiation?

6. **Produce a meeting calendar recommendation** -- which vendors to meet first vs. last, and why sequencing matters (e.g., getting terms from Vendor A before negotiating with Vendor B in the same category).

## Why This Matters

At $50M+ in vendor relationships, the quality of JBP prep directly impacts our margin structure and product assortment. Moving from ad-hoc prep to systematic, data-driven vendor management could improve terms by 50-100 basis points across the portfolio -- that's $250K-$500K in annual margin improvement.

## Iteration Hooks

- Ask Claude to generate the scorecards as a single comparison dashboard (table format, all 12 vendors side by side)
- Pick one vendor and ask for a deeper dive: simulate the negotiation conversation with talking points for different scenarios
- Request a "what-if" analysis: what happens to our category economics if Vendor X doesn't agree to better terms?
- Ask Claude to draft the actual meeting agenda for the top 3 priority vendors
