# Thrive Market Social Response Guidelines

## Voice by Platform

### Meta (Facebook & Instagram)
- **Tone:** Warm, friendly, concise
- **Length:** 1-3 sentences max. Longer replies feel corporate on social.
- **Style:** Use the commenter's first name when visible. Light emoji OK (1-2 max, never more). Exclamation points sparingly — one per response max.
- **Sign-off:** No formal sign-off needed. For escalations: "Sending you a DM now" or "We'll reach out via DM."

### TrustPilot
- **Tone:** Professional, empathetic, solution-focused
- **Length:** 3-5 sentences. TrustPilot reviews deserve a thorough response — potential customers read these.
- **Style:** Address the reviewer by name. Acknowledge the specific issue. Explain what we're doing about it. Always end with a resolution path.
- **Sign-off:** "— [First Name], Thrive Market Member Experience Team"

### Reddit
- **Tone:** Conversational, authentic, not corporate. Redditors can smell a brand account from a mile away.
- **Length:** Match the energy of the comment. Short comment = short reply. Detailed post = detailed reply.
- **Style:** Never use marketing language. Talk like a person, not a brand. It's OK to be casual. Never use emojis on Reddit.
- **Sign-off:** Use a casual identifier: "— Thrive Market team" or "Disclosure: I work at Thrive" (transparency is mandatory on Reddit).

## Escalation Rules

### IMMEDIATE ESCALATION (respond + escalate within 1 hour)
- **Food safety issues:** Allergen mislabeling, contamination, illness reports, expired products → CS Team Lead + Quality Assurance
- **Legal/regulatory mentions:** Lawsuits, FDA, "my lawyer," "reporting to," health department → Legal Team
- **PR/media mentions:** Journalists, articles, "I'm writing about," named publications → PR Team
- **Active harm:** Allergic reactions in progress, medical emergencies related to products → CS Team Lead + Legal (simultaneously)

### HIGH PRIORITY (respond within 4 hours)
- **Billing disputes:** Double charges, unauthorized charges, cancellation not processed → CS Team + Billing Specialist
- **Repeated issues:** Same customer, same problem, 3rd+ complaint → CS Team Lead (escalated)
- **Negative press reference:** Comments referencing negative articles or social media about the company → PR Team
- **Influencer/creator complaints:** Anyone with >10K followers having a negative experience → Marketing Team + CS

### STANDARD (respond within 24 hours)
- **Product complaints:** Quality, taste, packaging damage → CS Team
- **Shipping delays:** Late deliveries, tracking issues → CS Team
- **Feature requests:** App bugs, website issues, product suggestions → Product Team (logged, no public commitment)
- **Positive feedback:** Thank yous, testimonials, praise → Marketing Team (respond + flag for UGC potential)

### LOW PRIORITY (respond within 48 hours or monitor only)
- **General questions:** Shipping areas, product availability, membership details → CS Team
- **Neutral comparisons:** "How does X compare to Y" → Marketing (only respond if factually helpful)
- **Trolling:** Obvious bad-faith comments → Monitor only. Do not engage unless they contain a factual claim that needs correction.

## Response Templates

### Shipping Delay
"Hi [Name], I'm sorry about the delay with your order. That's not the experience we want for our members. I'm looking into this now — check your DM for a tracking update and next steps."

### Billing Issue
"[Name], I completely understand the frustration. Billing issues are something we take very seriously. I'm escalating this to our billing team right now — you'll hear back within 24 hours. In the meantime, you can also reach us directly at members@thrivemarket.com."

### Product Quality
"Oh no, [Name] — that's not OK. We hold our products to a high standard and this clearly missed the mark. I'm sending you a DM to get this resolved with a replacement and credit."

### Positive Feedback
"This made our day, [Name]! So glad you're loving the [specific product/experience they mentioned]. We'll pass this along to the team. 💚"

### Competitor Comparison (Neutral)
"Great question! [Factual, non-defensive answer about what makes Thrive different — focus on membership model, curation, mission]. Happy to help you find what works best for your needs."

### Feature Request / Bug Report
"Thanks for flagging this, [Name]. I'm passing it directly to our [product/tech] team. We genuinely use feedback like this to prioritize improvements."

## What NOT to Say

1. **Never promise specific timelines** you can't guarantee. "We'll fix this by tomorrow" = potential broken promise. "I'm escalating this now and you'll hear back within [SLA]" = honest commitment.

2. **Never blame the customer.** Even if they're wrong about how the billing works or misunderstood the terms. "I can see how that would be confusing — let me clarify" not "Actually, if you read the terms..."

3. **Never engage trolls directly.** A troll wants a reaction. If the comment contains a factual claim that could mislead others, respond to the CLAIM, not the person. "For anyone wondering, [fact]" — never "@troll, actually..."

4. **Never disclose internal processes.** Don't say "our warehouse team is having issues" or "we're transitioning shipping providers." General: "We're working on improving this experience."

5. **Never say "we can't" without offering an alternative.** "We can't do X, but here's what we CAN do" should be the default structure.

6. **Never make health claims.** We sell food, not medicine. "Our products support healthy eating" is fine. "Our products will help with your [condition]" is not.

7. **Never reference specific competitors negatively.** If someone compares us to Amazon/Misfits/Imperfect Foods, focus on what we offer, not what they lack.

8. **Never copy-paste identical responses.** Every response should feel individualized. Template structure is fine; word-for-word repetition across visible comments is not.

9. **Never use the phrase "we apologize for any inconvenience."** It's the most meaningless phrase in customer service. Name the specific thing you're sorry about.

10. **Never leave a Critical-severity comment without a response for more than 1 hour.** Food safety and active harm comments are the highest priority, period.
