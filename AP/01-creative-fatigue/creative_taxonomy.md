# Thrive Market Creative Diversity Matrix

## Message Framing Categories

### 1. Problem / Solution
Position Thrive as the answer to a specific pain point. Lead with the friction, resolve with the product.
- "Organic groceries are too expensive" → membership savings
- "I can't find [dietary need] options at my store" → curated selection
- "Grocery shopping takes my whole Saturday" → delivery convenience

### 2. Identity
Speak to who the customer is (or wants to be). The product is an expression of their values or lifestyle.
- "I'm the kind of person who reads labels"
- "Feeding my family clean food is non-negotiable"
- "I care about what goes into my body — and what goes into the planet"

### 3. Value / Savings
Lead with the economics. Membership model = wholesale pricing on premium products.
- Direct price comparisons (Thrive vs. Whole Foods vs. Amazon)
- Annual savings calculations
- Cost-per-meal breakdowns for families
- Free shipping thresholds and membership ROI

### 4. Mission Storytelling
Thrive's social impact and sustainability mission as the hero. Every membership funds a free one for someone in need.
- One-for-one membership model
- Carbon neutral shipping
- B Corp certification journey
- Supplier and farmer partnerships
- Plastic offset programs

## Visual Treatment Types

### A. UGC (User-Generated Content)
Real customers or creators showing authentic product use. Phone-shot aesthetic. High relatability.
- Unboxing hauls
- Pantry restocks
- "What I eat in a day" formats
- Recipe walkthroughs
- Before/after pantry makeovers

### B. Studio
Clean, controlled product photography. White or light backgrounds. Hero product shots.
- Single product hero with ingredient callouts
- Bundle/collection flat lays
- Price comparison split screens
- Membership savings calculators (graphic overlay)

### C. Lifestyle
Aspirational but attainable scenes of real life. Warm lighting, natural settings.
- Family kitchen scenes
- Meal prep sequences
- Morning routines
- Outdoor/active lifestyle with Thrive products integrated

### D. Mashup
Combines multiple visual styles or formats. Pattern interrupts.
- UGC testimonial + studio product shot
- Lifestyle scene + savings overlay
- Split screen: store shopping vs. Thrive delivery
- Creator narration over product B-roll

## Audience Angles

### i. Dietary Need
People searching for specific dietary solutions. High intent, specific product needs.
- Keto / low-carb
- Whole30 / Paleo
- Gluten-free
- Vegan / plant-based
- Allergen-free (nut-free, dairy-free, etc.)
- Postpartum nutrition
- Anti-inflammatory
- Mediterranean diet
- Gut health

### ii. Sustainability
Environmentally conscious consumers. Values-driven purchase decisions.
- Zero waste lifestyle
- Plastic-free packaging
- Carbon footprint reduction
- Ethical sourcing
- B Corp values alignment
- Regenerative agriculture supporters

### iii. Value Seeker
Price-sensitive shoppers who want premium products at better prices. Membership ROI focused.
- Budget-conscious families
- Bulk buyers
- Coupon/deal seekers upgrading to organic
- Cost-per-serving calculators
- Referral program users

### iv. GLP-1 / Health Journey
People on GLP-1 medications (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) who need specific nutritional support.
- High-protein, portion-controlled meals
- Clean ingredients for sensitive digestion
- Meal prep for medication schedules
- Nutrient-dense foods for reduced appetite
- Community support and shared journeys

---

## Diversity Rules

When building a creative rotation:
1. **No two consecutive ads** should use the same message frame
2. **Each weekly batch** should include at least 3 of 4 visual treatments
3. **Audience angles** should rotate — never run more than 40% of spend against a single angle
4. **Format mix** should maintain at least 25% video, 25% static, and the rest split between UGC and carousel
5. **New creatives** should deliberately fill gaps in the current matrix — check which cells are empty before briefing
