# Competitive Signals — Week 15 (April 7-13, 2026)

## Direct Competitors

### Imperfect Foods
- **Launched TikTok campaign** (spotted April 8): Series of "ugly produce" taste-test videos with micro-influencers (10K-50K followers). High engagement — top video hit 2.1M views in 4 days. Messaging: "Perfect taste, imperfect looks, incredible prices."
- **New positioning shift:** Moving from "reduce food waste" to "affordable organic" — encroaching on Thrive's value territory. Their landing page now leads with price comparison vs. Whole Foods (previously led with sustainability).
- **Impact assessment:** Likely competing for the same value-seeker audience on Meta/TikTok. Could explain slight CPM increases we're seeing on Meta dietary-need targeting.

### Misfits Market
- **Running 40% off first box promotion** (since April 5): Aggressive paid social push on Meta and Instagram. Ad creative focuses on "Why pay more for organic?" with side-by-side grocery receipt comparisons.
- **Expanded product catalog:** Added 200+ pantry staples and household items. Moving from produce-only to full grocery — directly competitive with Thrive's core assortment.
- **Free membership trial:** Now offering 30-day free membership (vs. Thrive's annual model). Lower barrier to entry for price-sensitive shoppers.
- **Impact assessment:** The 40% promotion is likely inflating CPAs across Meta for organic grocery keywords. Our value-seeker CPA may be elevated this week due to auction competition.

### Vitacost (Kroger-owned)
- **Quiet rebrand** spotted this week: New logo, refreshed packaging, "Clean living delivered" tagline. Looks like Kroger is investing more heavily in the DTC health/wellness channel.
- **Launched same-day delivery** in Miami and LA markets (new). Previously 3-5 day shipping only.
- **Impact assessment:** Low immediate threat — their awareness is low and they're still building. But Kroger's distribution muscle is worth watching over the next 6 months.

## Adjacent Competitors

### Amazon Fresh
- **Expanded same-day delivery** to 3 new markets this week: Austin, Nashville, Portland. Now covers 28 metro areas.
- **"Subscribe & Save" push:** New ad campaign highlighting 15% off recurring orders on organic/natural products. Running on Amazon DSP + Google SEM.
- **Private label expansion:** Amazon's "365 by Whole Foods" brand added 45 new organic pantry items this quarter.
- **Impact assessment:** Amazon's convenience + price play is the biggest long-term structural threat. Members who primarily value convenience (vs. mission/curation) are most at risk. Our Google SEM costs for "organic grocery delivery" have risen ~12% QoQ, likely driven by Amazon's bidding.

### Instacart
- **Partnered with Sprouts Farmers Market** for exclusive online storefront. Members get free delivery on $35+ orders.
- **GLP-1 marketing push:** New "Healthy Choices" collection featuring GLP-1-friendly products across partner stores. Running Meta ads targeting health/diet interest segments.
- **Impact assessment:** Instacart's GLP-1 move validates our thesis but also crowds the space. They're not DTC but they're targeting the same audience with a different model (multi-retailer marketplace vs. our curated membership).

## Market Context

### Macro Trends (This Week)
- **CPI data** (released April 10): Grocery inflation at 2.1% YoY, down from 2.8% last month. Organic category inflation at 1.4% — organic is closing the price gap with conventional. Good for our "organic doesn't have to be expensive" messaging.
- **GLP-1 market news:** Novo Nordisk announced expanded insurance coverage for Wegovy. Analyst estimates: 5M+ new GLP-1 users in 2026. Our fastest-growing audience segment is about to get a lot bigger.
- **Earth Month coverage:** Major outlets (NYT, CNN) running sustainability features. Organic search volume for "sustainable shopping" up 34% WoW. Tailwind for our Earth Day campaign next week.

### Platform Changes
- **Meta:** Algorithm update favoring longer-form Reels (30-60 sec) over short clips. Our 15-second UGC may see reduced distribution. Monitor Reels performance closely.
- **Google:** Q1 Performance Max updates — better attribution for view-through conversions. May explain some of the SEM improvement we're seeing (attribution methodology change vs. actual performance lift).
- **TikTok:** No major algorithm changes, but CPMs on food/health verticals up ~8% MoM industry-wide. Imperfect Foods' viral content may be contributing to category competition.

## Signals to Watch

1. **Misfits Market 40% promo duration** — if they extend past April 30, it signals margin pressure or growth-at-all-costs pivot
2. **Amazon Fresh expansion pace** — next announced markets will indicate if they're accelerating or plateauing
3. **Instacart GLP-1 collection performance** — if they start running Meta ads against GLP-1 keywords, our CPAs will increase
4. **Imperfect Foods positioning shift** — if they fully pivot to "affordable organic" vs. "reduce waste," they become a more direct competitor
