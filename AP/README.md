# Amina Pasha (CMO) -- Exercise Package

## Facilitator Guide

**Participant:** Amina Pasha, Chief Marketing Officer, Thrive Market
**Context:** DTC membership e-commerce (organic/natural groceries). Marketing team runs 17+ AI tools. Creative team saves ~40 hrs/week with AI. Full ACQ/BD team onboarded Claude CoWork April 2026.
**Time per exercise:** 30-45 minutes each
**Total session time:** ~2 hours (with transitions)

---

## Exercise Overview

| # | Exercise | Core Skill | Time |
|---|----------|-----------|------|
| 01 | Creative Fatigue Detector & Brief Generator | Pattern detection + structured creative output | 30-45 min |
| 02 | Campaign Performance Narrative | Data synthesis + executive communication | 30-45 min |
| 03 | Community Response Triage System | Classification + brand-voice writing at scale | 30-45 min |

---

## Pre-Session Setup

1. Each exercise folder contains a `prompt.md` and 2-4 synthetic data files
2. Participant pastes the prompt into Claude Code, then attaches or references the data files
3. All data is synthetic but modeled on realistic DTC membership e-commerce metrics
4. Data files cross-reference each other within each exercise (e.g., ad names in performance data match taxonomy categories)

## Running Each Exercise

### Exercise 01: Creative Fatigue Detector & Brief Generator
**Why this matters for Amina:** Her team tests 10-15 variants/week and wants to scale to 100+/month. The bottleneck is detecting fatigue early and generating on-brand replacement briefs without burning creative cycles.

**What to watch for:**
- Does Claude correctly flag the fatiguing creatives (declining CTR, rising CPA, high frequency)?
- Are the replacement briefs genuinely diverse across the taxonomy matrix?
- Does the rotation schedule account for audience saturation?

**Iteration hooks to suggest:**
- "Now add my actual Meta Ads export from last week"
- "Generate briefs specifically for our GLP-1 audience"
- "Create a Notion template for the creative rotation calendar"

### Exercise 02: Campaign Performance Narrative
**Why this matters for Amina:** The Member Engagement team already uses Cortex AI for weekly revenue narratives. This exercise builds the CMO-level Monday brief that synthesizes across channels and connects performance to strategy.

**What to watch for:**
- Does Claude distinguish between correlation and causation in performance shifts?
- Is the CEO summary genuinely concise and insight-dense?
- Does the competitive overlay add real strategic context or just noise?

**Iteration hooks to suggest:**
- "Now add our actual Looker export for this week"
- "Generate the brief as a Slack post I can drop in #marketing-leadership"
- "Add a section on budget reallocation recommendations"

### Exercise 03: Community Response Triage System
**Why this matters for Amina:** Community management consumes 40+ hrs/week across social and TrustPilot. They are evaluating Sprinklr/Emplifi. This exercise shows what AI-native triage looks like before buying another SaaS tool.

**What to watch for:**
- Are severity classifications defensible? (safety issues = Critical, billing = High, etc.)
- Do draft responses sound like Thrive, not like a chatbot?
- Does routing match the team_routing.md rules?

**Iteration hooks to suggest:**
- "Now run this on our actual TrustPilot export from last week"
- "Build a dashboard that updates hourly from our social feeds"
- "Add sentiment trend analysis across the last 30 days"

## Debrief Prompts

After completing all three exercises, use these questions:

1. **Scale question:** "Which of these three would save the most hours if deployed on real data this week?"
2. **Integration question:** "How would you connect this to your existing 17 AI tools?"
3. **Team question:** "Who on your team would own this workflow, and what would their Monday morning look like?"
4. **Stretch question:** "What if all three ran automatically every Monday at 6am and landed in your inbox?"
