# Thrive Market — Workforce Planning Scenarios (12-Month Horizon)

*Planning period: Q2 2026 through Q1 2027*

---

## Scenario 1: Base Case (No AI Acceleration)

**Assumptions:**
- 15% revenue growth ($500M → $575M)
- AI adoption stays at current levels — no new initiatives beyond what's already in production
- Headcount grows proportionally to revenue where needed
- Natural attrition rate: 12% annually (industry average for e-commerce)

**What this means:**
- Member Services needs ~8 additional agents to handle 15% more contacts (assuming no Sierra expansion)
- Operations needs ~4 additional warehouse associates plus 1 additional supervisor for volume growth
- Marketing needs 2 additional headcount for expanded campaigns
- Finance needs 1 additional analyst for reporting complexity at higher revenue
- Engineering needs 3 additional engineers for platform scaling
- Total new hires needed: ~20-22 net new headcount
- Projected total headcount: ~810-815

**Key tension:** Revenue is growing but margins are under pressure. Adding 20+ heads at an average fully-loaded cost of $95K means $1.9M+ in new compensation. Board is pushing for operating leverage, not linear headcount scaling.

---

## Scenario 2: AI-Accelerated (Current Roadmap, Full Deployment)

**Assumptions:**
- Same 15% revenue growth ($500M → $575M)
- All 27 planned AI initiatives execute on roadmap timelines
- Initiative success rate: 70% (some will underperform or delay)
- AI tool spend increases from $1.3M to $1.8M (Claude scaling, Workday, Syndigo)
- Natural attrition: 12% annually — but we strategically don't backfill AI-affected roles
- Retraining budget: $150K (external training, certifications, transition time)

**What this means:**
- Member Services: Sierra expansion from 68% → 82% (conservative vs. 88% target) means ~6 fewer agents needed. Through attrition (expect ~6 departures naturally), we don't backfill. Net: flat headcount despite 15% growth.
- Finance: Month-End Close Agent and AP automation save ~2 FTEs. Don't backfill 1 AP departure + retrain 1 into Financial Operations Analyst. Net: -1 vs. base case.
- Legal & People Ops: Contract accelerator + Workday + Policy Bot save ~1.5 FTEs. Don't backfill 1 TA Coordinator and 1 Paralegal when they depart. Net: -2 vs. base case.
- Operations: Mission Control + Supply Planning save ~1 FTE in analyst roles. Still need warehouse associates for volume. Net: hire 3 fewer than base case.
- Merchandising: Novi replacement + vendor scorecards save ~1 FTE. Retrain 2 compliance analysts into AI quality specialists. Net: flat.
- Marketing: AI creative pipeline means the team produces 2x output without adding headcount. Net: hire 0 instead of 2.
- Engineering: AI coding tools offset need for 2 of the 3 planned hires. But add 1 new AI Platform Engineer role. Net: hire 2 instead of 3.
- Tech Ops: Syndigo PIM + offboarding automation save ~0.5 FTE. Don't backfill 1 PIM specialist departure. Net: -1 vs. base case.
- Product: Sprint automation frees PM time but doesn't reduce headcount. Net: no change.

**Net result:**
- New hires needed: 5-7 (vs. 20-22 in base case)
- Roles eliminated through attrition: 12-14 (not backfilled)
- New AI-adjacent roles created: 3-4 (AI Quality Specialists, AI Platform Engineer, Knowledge Engineer)
- Projected total headcount: ~785-790 (vs. ~810-815 in base case)
- Net savings vs. base case: ~$1.5M in compensation + operating leverage

**New roles that emerge:**
| Role | Function | Why It Exists |
|------|----------|--------------|
| AI Quality Specialist (x2) | Merchandising, Member Services | Reviews AI outputs, manages AI tool calibration, ensures quality standards |
| AI Platform Engineer | Engineering | Maintains Claude Enterprise deployment, builds internal AI tooling, governs API access |
| Knowledge Engineer | Engineering/Product (shared) | Designs and maintains Project Genome knowledge graph; cross-functional data architecture |
| Financial Operations Analyst | Finance | Former AP specialist; now manages AI-assisted workflows and exception handling |
| Member Insight Analyst (x2) | Member Services | Former QA analysts; now derive strategic insights from AI-processed interaction data |
| Personalization Strategist | Marketing | Former CRM specialist; now manages AI-driven personalized member communications |

## Scenario 3: Aggressive AI (Stretch Growth + Maximum AI Leverage)

**Assumptions:**
- 25% revenue growth ($500M → $625M) — driven by new member acquisition and higher basket sizes
- AI enables this growth without proportional headcount — the entire thesis is operating leverage
- All 27 initiatives execute, plus 5 additional high-impact initiatives identified during execution
- Initiative success rate: 80% (aggressive execution focus)
- AI tool spend increases from $1.3M to $2.4M (significant new investment in Sierra expansion, custom AI tools, additional Claude capacity)
- Natural attrition: 12%, and we proactively manage 2-3 additional transitions
- Retraining budget: $300K (larger investment in upskilling)

**What this means:**
- Member Services: Sierra reaches 88% target. Volume grows 25% but AI absorbs all growth plus current workload. Reduce team from 48 to 36 agents (natural attrition + voluntary transitions with severance). Add 3 AI Quality Specialists. Net: -9 agents, +3 specialists.
- Finance: Full automation suite operational. Team of 15 becomes team of 11. 4 tactical roles absorbed through attrition and retraining. Net: -4.
- Marketing: AI handles 60% of content production at first-draft quality. Same team drives 25% more revenue without any headcount growth. Net: flat but 2x productivity.
- Operations: Warehouse headcount still grows with volume (+8 associates) but all analytical/planning roles flat despite 25% growth. Net: +8 warehouse, -2 analytical.
- Engineering: AI coding tools enable 25% more output. Hire 2 AI-specialized engineers, don't backfill 3 natural departures. Net: -1.
- All other functions: Similar to AI-Accelerated but with more aggressive attrition management.

**Net result:**
- New hires needed: 12-15 (mostly warehouse associates for physical volume)
- Roles reduced: 20-24 (mix of attrition + managed transitions)
- New AI-adjacent roles created: 8-10
- Projected total headcount: ~775-780 (despite 25% revenue growth)
- Revenue per employee: grows from $625K to $800K+ (28% improvement)
- Net savings vs. base case: ~$3.5M in compensation + operating leverage
- Additional AI investment required: ~$1.1M over current spend

**The honest assessment of Scenario 3:**
This scenario delivers the best financial outcome but carries the highest execution risk and the highest human cost. Managed transitions (even with severance and support) affect real people. The narrative must be: "We're growing 25% and keeping the team roughly flat — that's AI enabling growth, not AI replacing people." But in Member Services specifically, it does mean fewer agents. The ethical obligation: generous transition packages, retraining into AI-adjacent roles where possible, and honest communication.

---

## Key Variables That Shift All Scenarios

1. **Natural attrition rate** — If attrition is lower than 12%, we either need to be more proactive about transitions or accept higher headcount. If higher, the math works even better.

2. **Sierra performance** — The Member Services headcount swings massively on whether Sierra hits 82% or 88%. Every percentage point is worth roughly 1 agent FTE.

3. **SOX/compliance resolution speed** — Finance initiatives are blocked until Legal and Compliance define SOX-compliant AI workflows. A 6-month delay shifts $280K in savings to the right.

4. **Retraining success rate** — If we can't successfully retrain tactical workers into AI-adjacent roles, we lose institutional knowledge and face morale problems.

5. **AI tool cost trajectory** — Claude costs are scaling fast. If we don't govern spend, the savings from headcount reduction get eaten by tool costs.
