# Functional AI Governance Requests

*What each function is asking for from a company-wide AI governance perspective. Compiled from the 9 functional AI strategy documents.*

---

## Operations
**Key ask: "AI Captains" and "AI Coaches" program**
- Wants designated AI champions in each team who receive advanced training and serve as first-line governance
- "AI Captains" would own tool adoption within their team, ensure quality standards, and escalate issues
- "AI Coaches" (more senior) would work cross-functionally to share best practices
- Also requesting: clear policy on when AI-generated work must be disclosed to leadership

## Merchandising
**Key ask: Tool consolidation and central vetting**
- Currently worried about tool proliferation — multiple overlapping subscriptions across teams
- Wants a formal process for evaluating new AI tools before purchase: "We need someone to say 'we already have a tool that does that'"
- Requesting a 30-day trial framework with defined success criteria before committing to annual contracts
- Specific concern: 5 tools with overlapping ingredient/compliance capabilities

## Engineering
**Key ask: "Paved path" for non-engineer AI app development**
- Non-engineering teams are building Claude-powered tools (Operations built Mission Control)
- Engineering wants a supported framework so these tools are built safely: approved patterns, security review, data access controls
- Also requesting: Claude Enterprise spend governance — who approves new seats, what's the budget?
- Concern: teams using Claude for infrastructure/security-adjacent tasks without security review

## Legal
**Key ask: Mandatory attorney review for all AI-generated legal outputs**
- Non-negotiable position: every AI output in legal work must be reviewed by a licensed attorney
- Wants formal policy distinguishing "AI as research assistant" from "AI as drafter" — different review levels
- Requesting company-wide AI hallucination awareness training — "other functions don't understand how confidently AI fabricates"
- Also wants: clear CCPA guidance on member data use in AI tools

## Finance
**Key ask: SOX-compliant AI workflow framework**
- Needs formal guidance on how AI can be used in SOX-controlled processes
- Wants clear definitions: What does "human review" mean? Can AI prepare if human reviews? Can AI flag if human validates?
- Requesting: AI audit trail requirements — every AI-assisted financial output must have a documented review chain
- Blocked: Month-End Close Agent, AP automation, and bank file processing all waiting on this framework

## Marketing
**Key ask: Centralized tool vetting and content quality standards**
- 17 AI tools across the marketing function — wants help rationalizing
- Requesting: AI content quality standards to prevent "AI slop" — clear criteria for when AI-generated content is good enough
- Wants: health/nutrition claims review checklist specifically for AI-generated product content
- Also requesting: brand voice guidelines for AI tools — "AI doesn't sound like Thrive Market yet"

## Member Services
**Key ask: Sierra vendor management and AI QA standards**
- 68% of contacts through Sierra — wants formal SLA for knowledge base sync (currently no SLA, leading to wrong information)
- Requesting: monthly AI QA audit cadence (currently doing 660 reviews/month but with no formal framework)
- Wants: escalation policy — clear criteria for when AI should hand off to a human agent
- Concern: single-vendor dependency — "what happens if Sierra goes down for a day?"

## Tech Ops
**Key ask: AI-safe compliance workflows**
- SOX and PCI compliance require audit trails — AI-assisted processes need to maintain them
- Wants: formal AI involvement documentation in all compliance-relevant workflows
- Requesting: human-in-the-loop policy for AI-triggered access management (offboarding incident)
- Specific ask: 48-hour confirmation hold before any AI-triggered system access changes

## Product
**Key ask: AI integration with product development lifecycle**
- 37% of PM time on sprint management — wants AI to reduce this but needs clear policy on AI in product decisions
- Requesting: guidelines on AI-generated competitive intelligence — must include verification of data currency
- Wants: framework for evaluating AI features in product roadmap (Figma Make, product knowledge graph)
- Concern: AI-generated PRDs and specs may embed assumptions that no one verifies
