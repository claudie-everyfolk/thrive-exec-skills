# Thrive Market — Contract Negotiation Playbook

**Version:** 3.2
**Last Updated:** May 2026
**Owner:** Office of the General Counsel

---

## Purpose

This playbook defines Thrive Market's standard negotiating positions for vendor and SaaS agreements. All contract reviewers must apply these positions during initial review. Deviations require approval per the Risk Tolerance Framework.

---

## 1. Liability Cap

**Standard Position:** Provider's total aggregate liability shall be capped at the greater of (a) the fees paid by Customer during the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the claim, or (b) $500,000.

**Mutual Application:** Liability caps must apply equally to both parties. Asymmetric liability provisions are not acceptable without GC approval.

**Carve-outs:** The following are standard carve-outs from the liability cap (liability is uncapped for these):
- Provider's indemnification for IP infringement
- Breach of confidentiality obligations
- Provider's gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Data breach caused by Provider's failure to maintain required security standards

**Red Line:** Customer's liability shall never be unlimited for any category of obligation.

---

## 2. Indemnification

**Standard Position:** Mutual indemnification only. Each party indemnifies the other for:
- Third-party IP infringement claims arising from that party's technology or content
- Breach of confidentiality
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct

**Customer Indemnification Scope:** Customer will indemnify for claims arising directly from Customer Data content (e.g., if Customer uploads infringing material). Customer will NOT indemnify for:
- Regulatory investigations or enforcement actions (these are the cost of doing business; each party bears its own)
- Claims arising from outputs or derived insights generated by the vendor's platform
- Acts or omissions of Authorized Users beyond Customer's reasonable control
- Vendor's own business operations, affiliates, or successors

**Red Line:** Broad, catch-all indemnification language that extends Customer's exposure beyond direct actions is not acceptable.

---

## 3. Term and Renewal

**Standard Position:** Initial term of one (1) year with automatic renewal for successive one (1) year periods.

**Auto-Renewal Notice Period:** Either party may elect not to renew by providing written notice at least thirty (30) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

**Maximum Commitment:** No initial term longer than one (1) year without VP-level business sponsor approval. No auto-renewal term longer than one (1) year.

**Red Line:** Auto-renewal with notice periods exceeding sixty (60) days is not acceptable. Initial terms exceeding two (2) years require GC approval.

---

## 4. Termination for Convenience

**Standard Position:** Either party may terminate for convenience upon thirty (30) days' written notice.

**Refund on Early Termination:** If Customer terminates for convenience, Provider shall refund a pro-rata portion of any prepaid fees for the unused portion of the current term.

**Red Line:** Termination for convenience must be mutual. Agreements where only the vendor can terminate for convenience are not acceptable.

---

## 5. Data Processing and Privacy

**Standard Position:**
- Provider must process Customer Data in compliance with CCPA, all applicable state privacy laws, and Thrive Market's Data Processing Addendum (DPA)
- Provider must execute Thrive Market's standard DPA or an equivalent that includes all required provisions
- Data processing locations must be disclosed and limited to the United States unless explicitly approved
- Provider must support Consumer Rights Requests (access, deletion, opt-out) within ten (10) business days
- Subprocessor changes require thirty (30) days advance notice with meaningful objection rights (not limited to termination)

**Data Retention Post-Termination:**
- Provider must delete or return all Customer Data within thirty (30) days of termination
- No retention of Customer Data in any form (including aggregated/anonymized) after termination unless explicitly agreed in writing

**Data License:**
- Provider may use Customer Data only for the purpose of delivering the contracted services
- No secondary use (benchmarking, product improvement, marketing, model training) without explicit opt-in consent
- Any aggregation/anonymization rights must be narrowly scoped and revocable

**Red Line:** Broad perpetual licenses to Customer Data, data retention beyond 30 days post-termination, or unilateral Privacy Policy changes affecting Customer Data are not acceptable.

---

## 6. Intellectual Property

**Standard Position:**
- All custom deliverables, integrations, configurations, and work product created specifically for Customer in the performance of Professional Services shall be owned by Customer ("Work Product")
- Provider retains ownership of its pre-existing IP and the Platform
- Customer grants Provider no license to Customer's pre-existing IP beyond what is necessary to deliver the services
- Feedback clauses are acceptable if limited to non-confidential suggestions

**Red Line:** Vendor ownership of custom work product created for Customer is not acceptable. Overly broad IP assignment or licensing from Customer to Provider is not acceptable.

---

## 7. Price Escalation

**Standard Position:** Fee increases upon renewal shall not exceed four percent (4%) annually, with notice at least ninety (90) days before the renewal date.

**Red Line:** Uncapped price escalation or increases exceeding six percent (6%) annually are not acceptable without CFO approval.

---

## 8. Dispute Resolution

**Standard Position:** Governing law shall be the State of California. Disputes shall be resolved in state or federal courts located in Los Angeles County, California.

**Acceptable Alternative:** Binding arbitration is acceptable if conducted in Los Angeles, California under AAA Commercial Arbitration Rules with a single arbitrator.

**Red Line:** Arbitration outside of California or under unfamiliar arbitration bodies requires GC review.

---

## 9. Assignment

**Standard Position:** Neither party may assign without consent. No exceptions for mergers, acquisitions, or asset sales — all assignments require prior written consent from the non-assigning party.

**Red Line:** Unilateral assignment rights for the vendor (even in M&A context) are not acceptable without a change-of-control termination right for Customer.

---

## 10. Force Majeure

**Standard Position:** Standard force majeure clause is acceptable. Must include a mutual right to terminate if force majeure continues for more than ninety (90) days.

---

## 11. Warranty

**Standard Position:** Provider must warrant that: (a) the Platform will perform materially in accordance with documentation; (b) services will be performed in a professional manner; (c) the Platform will not infringe third-party IP rights; (d) Provider complies with all applicable laws.

**Red Line:** Broad warranty disclaimers that disclaim implied warranties of non-infringement are not acceptable for SaaS agreements.
