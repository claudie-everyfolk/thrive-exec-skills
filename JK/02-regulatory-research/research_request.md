# Internal Legal Research Request

**From:** Rachel Dominguez, VP of Marketing
**To:** Legal Department
**Date:** June 18, 2026
**Priority:** High — Q3 campaign launch targeted for August 4
**Re:** Legal Review of "Smart Savings" Summer Campaign Claims

---

## Background

We're planning our biggest campaign of the year — "Smart Savings Summer" — targeting both member acquisition and retention. The campaign centers on Thrive Market's value proposition: that our membership model delivers significant savings on organic and natural groceries compared to traditional retail.

The campaign will run across email (2.1M member list + acquisition), paid social (Meta, TikTok, YouTube), display (programmatic), website landing pages, and our mobile app. Total media spend: ~$2.8M over 8 weeks.

## Claims Requiring Legal Review

### Claim 1: Savings Comparison
We want to state: **"Thrive Market members save an average of $32 per order — that's over $500 a year on organic groceries compared to Whole Foods Market prices."**

Our data team calculated this by comparing 14 months of member purchase data (March 2025 – April 2026, n = 847,000 orders) against Whole Foods Market's publicly listed online prices for identical or equivalent products. The methodology was reviewed by our analytics team but has not been independently audited.

### Claim 2: Health Reference
We want to include in educational content (blog posts, email series, social posts): **"Studies show that switching to organic food can reduce pesticide exposure by up to 60%. At Thrive Market, we make that switch affordable."**

This references a 2019 study published in *Environmental Research* by Baudry et al. that found a significant reduction in urinary pesticide metabolites among participants who consumed primarily organic food. The study was conducted in France with ~700 participants.

### Claim 3: Member Data Usage
To calculate the savings figures, we used individual member purchase histories from our platform. We plan to also use aggregated purchase data to create audience segments for targeted advertising (e.g., "members who buy organic produce weekly" or "members who switched from conventional to organic in the last 6 months").

### Claim 4: California-Specific Advertising
Approximately 38% of our members are California residents. We need to ensure all claims comply with California-specific advertising regulations in addition to federal requirements.

## What We Need

1. **Substantiation requirements** for the savings claim under FTC guidelines — can we make this comparison? What do we need to have documented?

2. **Clinical study reference** — can we cite the Baudry et al. study? What disclaimers are needed? Are there risks in linking pesticide reduction to our product offering?

3. **CCPA implications** — are there issues with using member purchase data to calculate the savings figure and/or create targeted audience segments? Do we need to update our privacy notice?

4. **California advertising regulations** — anything beyond federal FTC requirements we need to worry about under California Business & Professions Code or other state-specific rules?

## Timeline

We need initial guidance by **June 25** to finalize creative briefs. Full legal review of final ad copy by **July 18** before the August 4 launch. If any claims need to be modified or dropped, we need to know ASAP so creative can adjust.
