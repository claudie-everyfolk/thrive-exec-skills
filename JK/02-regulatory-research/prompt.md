# Regulatory Research Accelerator

## Context Frame

I'm the General Counsel and Chief Administrative Officer at Thrive Market, an online membership-based grocery retailer specializing in organic and natural products. We adopted CoCounsel for legal research and cut turnaround from 2-3 hours to 30-45 minutes per research question. But hallucination remains my #1 concern with AI-assisted legal work — if an AI cites a regulation that doesn't exist or misstates a holding, that's a malpractice risk, not a productivity gain.

I've attached three files:
- `research_request.md` — an internal legal research request from our Marketing team about an upcoming campaign
- `prior_guidance.md` — two prior legal memos our team issued on related topics (one may be partially outdated)
- `regulatory_reference.md` — key regulatory provisions relevant to this analysis

Our Marketing team wants to move fast on a Q3 campaign. The claims involve comparative pricing, health-adjacent messaging, and use of member purchase data. This touches FTC, CCPA, and California advertising law — a real tangle.

## The Ask

1. **Structured legal memo**: Address all four questions from the research request in a formal memo format (To/From/Date/Re/Analysis/Conclusion). For each question, provide: the applicable legal framework, analysis of how it applies to the specific facts, and a clear recommendation.

2. **Risk assessment per claim**: For each marketing claim, provide a traffic-light rating: (a) Can Proceed — claim is substantiated and compliant as-is; (b) Proceed with Modifications — claim needs specific changes to be compliant; (c) Do Not Proceed — claim carries unacceptable legal risk. Explain the basis for each rating.

3. **Recommended disclaimer language**: Draft specific disclaimer text for any claim rated "Proceed with Modifications." The disclaimers should be practical for use in digital ads, email campaigns, and website landing pages.

4. **Hallucination flags**: This is the critical one. For every legal citation, regulatory reference, or statement of law in your analysis, flag your confidence level: (a) High — based on well-established, widely-cited authority; (b) Medium — based on reasonable interpretation but should be verified against primary source; (c) Low — extrapolated or uncertain, must be verified before relying on this. Explain specifically what a lawyer should check and where.

5. **Prior guidance comparison**: Review the two prior memos in `prior_guidance.md` and flag anything that may be outdated, superseded, or inconsistent with current law. Note any areas where our prior advice should be updated.

6. **Action items for Marketing**: Produce a plain-English summary of what Marketing can and can't do, with specific next steps they need to take before launching the campaign.

## Why This Matters

AI-assisted legal research is only valuable if the lawyer using it knows which parts to trust and which parts to verify. This exercise isn't about getting the research "right" — it's about building the habit of treating AI output as a first draft that accelerates human judgment rather than replacing it. The hallucination flagging in Ask #4 is the whole point.

## Iteration Hooks

- "Marketing pushed back — they say Whole Foods comparison is standard in the industry. Find me 3 examples of FTC enforcement actions against grocery/food companies for comparative advertising claims in the last 5 years."
- "Our privacy counsel flagged that we may need to update our CCPA notice before using purchase data this way. What specific disclosures would need to change?"
- "The CMO wants to know if we can make the same claims on TikTok and Instagram. Are there platform-specific advertising rules we need to consider?"
- "Rewrite the memo for a non-lawyer audience — the CMO needs to present this to the board."

## Stretch Goals

- Create a "Legal Research Confidence Checklist" — a reusable template for evaluating AI-generated legal research
- Build a regulatory monitoring framework: what sources should we watch for changes that would affect this campaign?
- Draft a research request intake form that captures enough context for AI to produce useful first-pass analysis
