# Employee Communications in Thrive's Voice

## Context Frame

I'm the General Counsel and Chief Administrative Officer at Thrive Market, an online membership-based grocery retailer. In addition to legal, I oversee People Ops — Talent Acquisition, HR, benefits, and employee communications. We're building a "Voice of Thrive" content foundation to standardize how we communicate with our workforce, but right now Claude outputs are inconsistent in tone, and prompting practices aren't standardized across the People Ops team.

I've attached three files:
- `comms_scenario.md` — three real communication scenarios at different sensitivity levels that we need to draft this week
- `thrive_voice_guide.md` — our internal communications style guide (the "Voice of Thrive")
- `audience_context.md` — our workforce composition, communication channels, and logistical context

These three scenarios span the spectrum: a benefits change that affects specific employees negatively, a major policy shift that will be controversial, and a positive celebration. Each requires a different touch, but all must sound like Thrive.

## The Ask

1. **Draft communications for all three scenarios**: Write the primary all-company communication for each scenario, fully tailored to Thrive's voice guide. Each should be ready to send — not a template, but actual copy with the specific details from the scenario files.

2. **Manager talking points**: For the two sensitive announcements (dental benefits change and return-to-office policy), create manager talking points — a structured guide that people managers can use in 1:1s and team meetings to address questions and concerns. Include what to say, what not to say, and how to handle pushback.

3. **FAQ documents**: For each of the two sensitive scenarios, create a comprehensive FAQ (8-12 questions each) covering the questions employees are most likely to ask. Write answers in Thrive's voice — honest, direct, empathetic.

4. **Channel strategy**: For each scenario, specify which channels to use (email, Slack, FC displays, manager cascades), in what order, and with what timing. Explain why each channel was chosen given the audience composition in `audience_context.md`.

5. **Tone comparison**: For one of the sensitive scenarios, write the same message twice — once in "generic corporate" voice and once in "Thrive voice." Annotate the differences to show the team exactly what makes Thrive's voice distinctive. This becomes a training artifact.

## Why This Matters

Thrive employs ~60% fulfillment center hourly workers who experience company communications very differently than HQ knowledge workers. A tone-deaf benefits change email can tank morale at the FCs in a day. An RTO announcement that reads like it was written by McKinsey will land like a brick. Getting the voice right isn't a nice-to-have — it's an operational requirement, especially at sites with union activity. This exercise tests whether AI can match a company's specific voice well enough to produce first drafts that People Ops can actually use.

## Iteration Hooks

- "The CEO wants to add a personal note to the Best Places to Work announcement. Draft 2-3 paragraphs in Nick Green's voice — optimistic, founder-energy, mission-driven."
- "Union stewards at the Batesville FC are asking for a separate communication about the dental change. Draft a version specifically for union-represented employees."
- "An employee posted the RTO announcement on Blind with negative commentary. Draft an internal Slack message from the CHRO acknowledging the discussion without being defensive."
- "Translate the dental benefits FAQ into Spanish for our FC workforce."

## Stretch Goals

- Create a "Voice of Thrive" prompt template that any People Ops team member could use for future communications
- Build a sensitivity scoring rubric: given a communication topic, automatically classify its sensitivity level and recommend the appropriate review chain
- Draft a communications calendar template for the quarter that sequences these three announcements optimally
