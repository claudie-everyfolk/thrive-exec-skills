## Scenario 1: Dental Benefits Change

**Sensitivity Level:** High
**Target Send Date:** Week of July 7, 2026
**Effective Date:** September 1, 2026 (60-day notice)

### What's Happening

Thrive Market is switching dental insurance providers from Delta Dental PPO to Guardian Dental DentalGuard Preferred. This is driven by a 22% premium increase from Delta Dental at renewal. Guardian's plan offers comparable coverage at 14% lower cost, saving Thrive approximately $1.2M annually.

### The Good

- Monthly employee premium contribution stays the same (no cost increase to Thrivers)
- Preventive care coverage is identical (100% for cleanings, exams, X-rays)
- Annual maximum increases from $1,500 to $2,000 per person
- Orthodontic coverage improves: lifetime max goes from $1,500 to $2,000
- Guardian's network includes 118,000 dentist access points nationwide

### The Bad

- Approximately 340 Thrivers (out of 2,100 enrolled) have a current dentist who is NOT in the Guardian network
- Transition requires re-enrollment; auto-enrollment is not available for the new plan
- Employees mid-treatment (orthodontics, multi-visit procedures) will need to either switch providers or pay out-of-network rates to continue with their current dentist
- The out-of-network reimbursement rate under Guardian (60%) is lower than Delta Dental's (70%)

### Key Details

- Open enrollment window: July 21 – August 15, 2026
- Benefits team will hold 4 info sessions (2 virtual for HQ/remote, 2 in-person at Batesville and LA FCs)
- A one-time "Provider Transition Stipend" of $250 is available for any Thriver whose current dentist is not in-network (to help cover costs of transitioning mid-treatment)
- HR is preparing a provider lookup tool so Thrivers can check if their dentist is in Guardian's network
- Benefits FAQ page will be updated on the internal wiki

### Stakeholders

- **Approved by:** CFO (cost savings), GC/CAO (compliance review), VP People
- **Drafted by:** People Ops with Legal review
- **Sensitive audience:** Thrivers at Batesville FC (largest site, ~480 employees, some union-represented)

## Scenario 2: Return-to-Office Policy Update

**Sensitivity Level:** Very High
**Target Send Date:** Week of July 14, 2026
**Effective Date:** October 1, 2026 (next quarter)

### What's Happening

Thrive Market is updating its flexible work policy for HQ employees (Marina del Rey office). The current policy is fully flexible — no required in-office days. The new policy requires a minimum of 3 days per week in-office (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday) for all HQ-based roles.

### Rationale

- Cross-functional collaboration has declined measurably since going fully flexible in 2023
- Product development cycle time has increased 30% (per Engineering & Product leadership)
- New hires report difficulty building relationships and learning the culture
- 67% of HQ managers reported in an internal survey that team cohesion has suffered
- CEO and leadership team believe in-person collaboration is essential for Thrive's next growth phase

### What's NOT Changing

- Fulfillment center employees: no change (FC roles have always been on-site)
- Remote employees (hired as remote): no change — existing remote roles remain remote
- Flexible Friday/Monday: employees can still work from home on Mondays and Fridays
- Core hours policy: no change (10am–4pm PT)

### Accommodations

- 90-day transition period (October 1 – December 31) with no strict enforcement
- Employees with documented medical/disability accommodations will continue to be accommodated
- Caregiving flexibility: employees with childcare or eldercare responsibilities can apply for a modified schedule (e.g., 2 days/week) through their manager + HR
- Commute stipend: $150/month for HQ employees to offset commute costs (parking, transit, gas)
- Office upgrades: new focus rooms, improved cafe menu, upgraded AV in conference rooms

### Stakeholders

- **Decided by:** CEO, COO, CHRO
- **Most affected:** ~320 HQ employees who have been working 0-2 days in office
- **Hot spots:** Engineering (most resistant in survey data), Design (most supportive), Customer Experience (mixed — partially remote team)

### Risks

- Attrition risk: 12-15% of HQ employees indicated in anonymous survey they would "seriously consider leaving" if required to come in 3+ days
- Glassdoor/Blind: high probability of negative posts
- Equity concern: FC workers may perceive this as HQ getting special treatment (they've always been on-site)
- California labor law: need to ensure commute time/cost expectations don't create wage and hour issues for non-exempt HQ employees

## Scenario 3: Best Places to Work Recognition

**Sensitivity Level:** Low (positive)
**Target Send Date:** As soon as possible (announcement embargo lifts July 1, 2026)
**External Announcement:** Coordinated with Comms/PR for same-day press release

### What's Happening

Thrive Market has been named to Built In's "Best Places to Work 2026" list in two categories:
- **Best Places to Work in Los Angeles (Large Companies)**
- **Best Benefits Programs**

This is the first time Thrive has appeared on the Built In list. The recognition is based on company data submitted in Q1 2026 covering: compensation, benefits, culture programs, DEI initiatives, remote/flexible work, and professional development.

### Why This Matters Internally

- Strong morale signal, especially given industry headwinds (DTC e-commerce contraction, recent layoffs at competitors)
- Recruiting value: Built In is a major tech talent platform; this recognition drives inbound applications
- Validates investments made in benefits and culture programs over the past 18 months
- CEO Nick Green wants to use this as a moment to reinforce Thrive's mission and thank the team

### Celebration Plans

- **All-hands mention:** CEO will reference in the July all-hands (July 3)
- **Slack celebration:** Dedicated post in #general with the Built In badge graphic
- **Swag:** Limited-edition "Best Place to Thrive" stickers for HQ and FC sites
- **FC recognition:** Posters for break rooms at Batesville and LA fulfillment centers
- **Social media:** Coordinated external posts (LinkedIn, Instagram) — employees encouraged to share
- **Team lunch:** Catered lunch at HQ and pizza/treats at FC sites on announcement day

### Tone

Celebratory but grounded. Proud without being boastful. Acknowledge that the award reflects the people, not the company abstractly. Avoid anything that feels out of touch given the broader economic context — some Thrivers may have friends at companies doing layoffs.
